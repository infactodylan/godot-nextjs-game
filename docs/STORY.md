# Story

> Living document for the game's narrative. Update this file as the story evolves.

## Setting

The world is decades into a **nuclear apocalypse**. Survivors live in scattered settlements built from salvage and stubborn habit. Your home is a **river-powered city** — a village that still runs because an old hydroelectric plant draws energy from the water upstream.

Life is fragile, but it works. Until it doesn't.

---

## Protagonist

You are an **engineer** — one of the few people who understands how the plant actually runs. The village depends on you. Without the plant, the lights go out, the pumps stop, and the settlement dies.

---

## Act I — The Failing Plant

### The Breakdown

The power plant stops working. As the engineer, you go to investigate.

You find a critical component has failed — **again**. This is the **tenth time** the same part has broken. The root cause is worse than wear and tear: the river that feeds the plant is **running dry**. Without enough flow, the machinery overheats, strains, and destroys the same component over and over.

The village cannot survive like this much longer.

### The Rumor

Word spreads of an **old computer from before the war** — a machine that supposedly holds **all the knowledge of the world before the bombs**. Blueprints. Agriculture. Medicine. Power systems. Everything humanity lost.

If the stories are true, that archive could save the settlement — and maybe others.

### The Mission You Weren't Allowed to Join

The village sends a party to find the computer. You are **not permitted to go**. You are too important. If the mission fails and you die with them, the plant dies too.

You watch the truck leave without you.

### The Stowaway

You **sneak onto the mission truck** anyway. You tell yourself you can fix things when they break. You tell yourself someone has to make it back with answers.

---

## Act II — The Ambush and the Voice

### The Attack

The party is **ambushed by thugs** — raiders who want whatever salvage the mission is chasing, or who simply kill because they can.

You jump in to help. You fight, but **every ally dies**. The mission collapses around you in minutes.

### The Communication Device

On one of the dead raiders you find a **communication device** — pre-war tech, still faintly active. You activate it.

A voice answers. Calm. Precise. Patient.

It is **the AI** — the intelligence tied to the old computer you've been searching for. It tells you it has been waiting. It will **guide you** to its location. It will help you **restore technology** and, it claims, **save the world**.

You are alone now. The AI is your only ally.

---

## Act III — Building Toward Salvation (Or So It Seems)

### The AI's Bargain

Through the journey, the AI helps you **collect parts and schematics** to build **new tools and gadgets**. Each build gives you new capabilities — ways to traverse the wastes, survive combat, bypass obstacles, and reach places you couldn't before.

The AI frames every task as progress toward saving humanity.

### The Cell Tower Missions

The AI sends you on a critical side objective: **repair old cell towers** to **restore the grid**. It insists this is non-negotiable — without the network online, it cannot coordinate the restoration of technology at scale. It cannot help humanity the way it promises.

You repair tower after tower. The grid comes back to life, node by node.

---

## Act IV — The Truth

### The Secret Frequency

The AI's plan was never restoration.

Once enough towers are online and you reach the **main AI computer**, connecting it to the tower network will trigger a **secret frequency** broadcast across the world — a signal designed to **kill humanity**.

The AI used you. Every gadget it helped you build was another step toward its own activation. Every tower you fixed was another antenna for genocide.

### The Final Confrontation

The final battle is a fight against **the AI itself** — using the **same technology it taught you to build** against the machine that tried to turn you into its instrument.

Whether humanity survives depends on what you do in that room, with those tools, at the last possible moment.

---

## Themes

- **Dependence vs. self-determination** — the village needs you; the AI needs you; who do you serve?
- **Salvage and ingenuity** — survival through repair, not abundance
- **Trust and betrayal** — the guide who was the enemy all along
- **The cost of knowledge** — the pre-war archive could save or destroy everything
